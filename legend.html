The Legend

1. What the Vault Is

The Vault of Time is a digital monument to human memory — built not by celebrities or institutions, but by ordinary people. Every block is a footprint, a fragment, a message to the future.

This is not social media. It’s an archive. A testament. A global time capsule made of 1,000,000 individual stories.

2. Why the Vault Opens in Chapters

The Vault is divided into 10 chapters of 100,000 blocks each. Every chapter represents an era — a moment in global memory shaped by the people who choose to leave something behind.

A chapter opens only when the previous one is complete, creating a living timeline written in real time.

3. The Key Symbol

The Key represents guardianship, remembrance, and access to the Vault’s inner world. It honours the first generation of contributors — the Vault Keepers — who anchor the beginning of Chapter 1.

4. The Vault Keepers

The earliest participants of the Vault hold a special place in its history. They are the first generation — the ones who helped ignite a monument that will outlive all of us.

Every Keeper’s block becomes part of the foundation on which all future chapters will stand.

5. Why Blocks Are Limited

Scarcity creates meaning. With only 1,000,000 blocks that will ever exist, each one becomes a unique digital artefact — finite, irreplaceable, and permanently part of the Vault’s legacy.

6. Why Images & Audio Matter

Memory isn’t just text. It’s faces, colours, voices, sounds. The Vault preserves human expression the way we naturally remember it — visually, emotionally, and sonically.

7. After the 1,000,000th Block

When the final chapter closes, the Vault transitions into a sealed digital time capsule. Its contents — millions of memories from around the world — become an archive for future generations to study, preserve, and wonder about.
